To enter numbers in scientific notation, use the following format:
a x 10^b
where "a" is the coefficient and "b" is the exponent. For example, the number 6,000,000 can be written in scientific notation as:
6 x 10^6
To enter a number in scientific notation with a negative exponent, use a negative sign before the exponent. For example, the number 0.000025 can be written in scientific notation as: 2.5 x 10^-5

A knot is a unit of speed used in navigation and aviation that measures the speed of a vessel or aircraft relative to the surrounding fluid (usually water or air). It is defined as one nautical mile per hour.
A nautical mile is a unit of distance used in navigation that is based on the circumference of the Earth. It is equal to one minute of latitude, which is approximately 1.852 kilometers or 1.151 statute miles.

The long ton, short ton, and metric ton are units of weight or mass used in different parts of the world.
The long ton, also known as the imperial ton or weight ton, is used in the United Kingdom and other countries in the Commonwealth of Nations. It is equal to 2,240 pounds or 1,016.05 kilograms.
The short ton, also known as the US ton or net ton, is used primarily in the United States. It is equal to 2,000 pounds or 907.18 kilograms.
The metric ton, also known as the tonne, is the standard unit of weight or mass used in most countries outside the United States and the Commonwealth of Nations. It is equal to 1,000 kilograms or 2,204.62 pounds.
